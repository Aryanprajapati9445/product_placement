Story Point 1

The Business Question

"Does product placement in stores actually impact sales?"

Context
  • Analyzing 989 products across 3 store positions: Aisle, End-cap, and Front of Store
  • 3 product categories examined: Clothing, Electronics, and Food
  • 4 consumer demographics: Families, College Students, Seniors, and Young Adults
  • Additional factors tracked: pricing, promotions, foot traffic, and seasonality
Story Point 2

Product Position & Sales Performance

"All three positions show similar average sales (~1,770 units)"

Key Insights
  • Aisle: 1,781 average sales — marginally the highest performer
  • Front of Store: 1,773 average sales — close second
  • End-cap: 1,754 average sales — slightly trailing
  • Surprising finding: position alone does NOT dramatically affect sales volume
Story Point 3

The Category Factor

"Product category interacts with placement to affect sales"

Key Insights
  • Different categories perform better in different positions
  • Category-position combinations reveal hidden patterns in the heatmap
  • Optimizing placement per category could unlock higher sales
  • The interaction effect matters more than position alone
Story Point 4

Demographics Matter

"Customer demographics influence how placement affects sales"

Key Insights
  • Different customer groups respond differently to product placement
  • Families, Seniors, College Students, and Young Adults show distinct patterns
  • Targeted placement strategies should vary per demographic segment
  • One-size-fits-all placement is sub-optimal
Story Point 5

Price & Promotion Effects

"Price competitiveness and promotions shape sales outcomes"

Key Insights
  • 99.9% of products are priced above competitors (avg +$2.47 premium)
  • Promotions have a measurable impact across different positions
  • Price-sales relationship varies by placement position
  • Combining promotions with strategic placement amplifies results
Story Point 6

The Multi-Factor View

"Foot traffic + promotion + position: the full picture"

Key Insights
  • Combining all factors reveals nuanced, multi-layered patterns
  • High foot traffic areas paired with promotions yield the best results
  • Seasonal effects influence placement effectiveness differently
  • Interactive dashboard exploration shows how factors work together
Story Point 7

Strategic Recommendations

"Data-driven recommendations for optimal product placement"

Recommendations
  • 1. Position alone is NOT the dominant sales driver — avoid over-investing in premium shelf space
  • 2. Focus on category-position optimization for maximum impact
  • 3. Target demographics with tailored placement strategies
  • 4. Combine promotions strategically with high-traffic positions
  • 5. Consider seasonal timing when making placement changes
  • 6. Monitor price competitiveness relative to competitors continuously

Results

Key Findings at a Glance

The most critical insights distilled from the analysis.

📊

Minimal Position Effect

Average sales across Aisle (1,781), Front of Store (1,773), and End-cap (1,754) differ by less than 1.5%, suggesting position alone is not the key driver.

🏷️

Price Premium Risk

99.9% of products are priced above competitors with an average premium of $2.47. This pricing gap may limit the impact of placement strategies.

🎯

Category Interaction

Product category significantly interacts with placement. Clothing, Electronics, and Food each respond differently to position changes.

👥

Demographic Variation

Families, Seniors, Young Adults, and College Students show distinct purchasing behaviors based on where products are placed.

📢

Promotion Multiplier

Promotions amplify the effect of specific positions. Combining the right promotion with the right placement can boost sales significantly.

🚶

Traffic-Position Synergy

High foot traffic areas combined with strategic placement and active promotions produce the strongest sales performance.
